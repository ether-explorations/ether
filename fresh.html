Inspiration

here’s some collected fragments of how freshness can be interpreted and applied:

  • “two pieces of ceramic touching each other after they haven’t for a while”
  • “a new jar of peanut butter”
  • “peeling the aluminium off a capsule of greek yogurt and licking it”
  • “petrichor. the verdant and wet aroma after a rain. it’s a sign of freshness, also decay. it only occurs where ancient, old, and dead organic material is turned over in the soil.” “lemons”

fresh is raw: it is playful, or renewed; but also an artifact of that which has dulled us to the point of needing to be renewed. like taking a morning stroll through a forest the week after retuing home for a loved one’s funeral and embracing the fragrance of flowers blooming when you didn’t even notice them start to bud.” “an abnegation of the old: both past and future
